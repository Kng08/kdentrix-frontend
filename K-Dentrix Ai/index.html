Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your visit to K-Dentrix.

How often should I visit the dentist? ▾
We recommend a check-up and professional cleaning every 6 months for most patients. Patients with gum disease or higher risk factors may need more frequent visits — our dentists will advise you personally.
Do you accept National Health Insurance (NHIS)? ▾
Yes! K-Dentrix is registered with the National Health Insurance Authority (NHIA). We also accept most private health insurance providers including Enterprise Health, Glico, and AXA Mansard. Contact us to confirm your specific plan.
What should I do in a dental emergency? ▾
Call our 24-hour emergency line immediately: 0503 756 324. For knocked-out teeth, rinse gently and keep the tooth moist (in milk or saliva). Come in as soon as possible — quick action significantly improves outcomes.
Is teeth whitening safe? ▾
When performed by a dental professional, teeth whitening is completely safe. Our in-chair whitening treatments use controlled concentrations of whitening agents and include custom-fitted trays that protect your gums throughout the process.
How long does a dental implant procedure take? ▾
The implant placement itself takes 1–2 hours per tooth under local anaesthetic. However, full osseointegration (healing) takes 3–6 months, after which the crown is placed. The entire process from consultation to final restoration is typically 4–8 months.
Do you offer flexible payment plans? ▾
Yes — for larger treatment plans (implants, orthodontics, full smile makeovers), we offer interest-free instalment payment options spread over 3–12 months. Ask our front desk team to set up a payment plan at your consultation.
Are your dental X-rays safe? ▾
Our modern digital X-ray equipment emits up to 90% less radiation than traditional film X-rays. We also use lead aprons and thyroid collars as standard protective measures. Dental X-rays are considered very safe for adults and children.
At what age should my child first visit the dentist? ▾
We recommend bringing your child for their first visit around their first birthday or within 6 months of their first tooth erupting. Early visits help establish healthy habits, allow us to monitor development, and make children comfortable with the dental environment.
